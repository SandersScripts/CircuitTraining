Circuit-Training

Lessons in coding, circuits, and problem solving

What I Can Do For You

Is your child interested in computers or you would like to introduce coding concepts to your son or daughter? Is camp over and now you want your children to have fun while learning valuable skills? Don't know where to begin?

I'm here to help! As an honors student in Accelerated Robotics and AP Computer Science student (as well as a published Minecraft designer), I can teach children ages 5-12 a variety of introductory and challenging computer and electronics skills.

I can help your child learn the fundamentals and have a solid grasp of computer science in a variety of fun and engaging ways. One popular method is using the game Minecraft to create an interactive space to explore circuits and logic gates using the "Redstone" functionality.

My proficiencies include:
  • -MIT's Scratch Creative Computing
  • -Minecraft Redstone
  • -Gamemaker
  • -Basic HTML, Java, Python, etc
  • -Basic Circuitry
  • -More advanced subjects available upon request

I charge $20 for one hour sessions. I offer a free 30 minute consultation to meet with you and/or your child in person or via Skype so you can get to know me and I can learn more about your son or daughter's abilities and interests. I find children generally lose focus after one hour, but longer sessions can be arranged if time, and especially interest, allows.

About Me

I have been interested in robotics, engineering and computers since my whole life. I am self-taught in a variety of computer languages such as Python, Java, JavaScript, CSS, and HTML. I am an active member of the Millburn High School Computer Science and State ranked Robotics clubs. I enjoy sharing my love of computers and electronics with others, and I hope to instill my passion for skills that are ever increasing in demand in today's technologically oriented world.

I am an avid Lego builder, and recently my work as a part of a Minecraft building team has been published in a book recommended by the [i]Wall Street Journal[/i].
